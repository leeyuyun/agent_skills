# Search Sources Catalog

Use this catalog to select high-quality search sources and processing resources.

## 0. Selection Logic

Apply this order:
1. Start with broad sources for recall.
2. Add domain sources for precision.
3. Add preprints for frontier coverage.
4. Add policy sources for governance claims.
5. Expand with citation chaining for missed clusters.

## 1. Core Scholarly Sources

Use at least two:
1. Google Scholar: broad coverage and citation chaining.
2. OpenAlex: open metadata graph and works API.
3. Crossref: DOI metadata and bibliographic normalization.
4. Semantic Scholar: relevance ranking and citation network.
5. Scopus or Web of Science: curated indexing for higher precision.

## 2. Domain-Specific Sources

Choose based on topic:
1. PubMed: medicine, health, and biomedical AI.
2. IEEE Xplore: engineering, signal processing, applied AI systems.
3. ACM Digital Library: computer science and HCI.
4. ACL Anthology: NLP and language technologies.
5. ERIC: education research and policy-oriented education studies.
6. PsycINFO: psychology and learning sciences.
7. SSRN: social science, law, and policy pre-publications.

## 3. Preprint Sources

Use for fast-moving fields:
1. arXiv: CS, math, physics, and quantitative methods.
2. medRxiv: medical preprints.
3. bioRxiv: life sciences preprints.

Mark preprints explicitly in evidence tables.

## 4. Policy and Standards Sources

Use when governance, ethics, or deployment policy is relevant:
1. OECD policy library.
2. UNESCO policy documents.
3. NIST AI Risk Management Framework resources.
4. EU AI Act and implementation guidance pages.
5. National education ministries and regulatory white papers.

## 5. Citation and Network Discovery

Use for expansion after seed papers:
1. Backward citation search: references used by seed papers.
2. Forward citation search: papers citing seed papers.
3. Co-citation and bibliographic coupling maps.
4. Related papers graph exploration.

## 6. Processing Resources (Recommended)

Use these resources to process large result sets:
1. Zotero: citation library, tagging, and deduplication.
2. Rayyan or Covidence: screening workflow support.
3. OpenRefine: metadata cleaning and normalization.
4. Spreadsheet or pandas workflow: evidence matrix construction.
5. DOI and URL resolvers: dead-link recovery and metadata completion.

## 7. Evidence Quality Frameworks (Recommended)

Use these to qualify synthesis confidence:
1. RoB 2 or ROBINS-I for primary intervention studies when applicable.
2. ROBIS or AMSTAR 2 for review-level quality appraisal.
3. GRADE for certainty of key claims.

Use fit-for-purpose quality checks by study type; do not force one tool on every source.

## 8. Source Mix Guidelines

For a default `30-40` citation review:
1. Peer-reviewed papers: 18-24.
2. High-quality preprints: 4-8.
3. Policy/standards/technical reports: 4-8.
4. Foundational older references: 2-4 when historically relevant.
